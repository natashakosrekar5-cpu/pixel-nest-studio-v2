01 / Strategic Opportunity

Editorial Spatial Rhythm for High-End Interiors

Luxury furniture buyers and interior architects do not shop like impulse e-commerce consumers. They evaluate spatial proportions, materials, and architectural context. The opportunity was to design a spacious editorial layout system that showcases furniture as architectural art.

02 / Design & Technical Constraints

Seamless Material & Finish Previews

Showing multiple wood finishes, marble veins, and textile swatches requires dynamic client-side rendering without triggering screen refreshes or heavy image swapping lags.

Design Evolution

What Needed To Change

Legacy Problem 01

Crowded Product Grids

Standard e-commerce templates crammed 4-5 items per row, choking spatial breathability.

Legacy Problem 02

Flat Material Samples

Textile swatches were hidden in tiny dropdown menus rather than interactive visual modules.

Legacy Problem 03

Generic Grid Rhythms

Lack of varied section sizes led to visual fatigue during long architectural portfolio browsing sessions.

Methodology

Design Process Timeline

PHASE 01

Architectural Benchmark

Analyzed leading architectural journals and luxury design house web flagships.

PHASE 02

Asymmetric Grid System

Created dynamic 12-column grid systems that alternate between full-bleed hero spreads and structured 2-column details.

PHASE 03

Material Selector UX

Engineered interactive material swatch selectors with instant live texture previews.

PHASE 04

Typographic Styling

Paired bold modern display typography (Syne) with clean geometric sans-serif (Inter).

PHASE 05

Responsive Optimization

Maintained strict spatial proportions across ultra-wide desktop monitors and mobile screens.

PHASE 06

Production Build

Hand-coded clean semantic HTML/CSS with modular component architecture.

Outcome

Final Experience & Strategic Impact

Architectural Impact

The website concept positioned Coco Republic as a premier interior design authority. Spatial layout hierarchy allowed visitors to appreciate the craftsmanship of each piece in context.

Design Takeaway

Whitespace is not empty space — it is an active architectural element. By giving furniture room to dominate the viewport, the digital medium mirrors physical gallery spaces.

"When space is designed with intention, objects transition from simple items into architectural statements."
